# Explainer Page Standard

## Purpose

Explainer Pages are factual, transparent documents designed to clarify the identity, purpose, and relationships of brands, organizations, and projects.

These pages exist to provide **clear public explanations** separate from marketing websites or promotional materials.

Each explainer page serves as a **reference document** that helps readers understand:

- what an organization or project is
- when it began
- who operates or owns it
- what it does
- how it relates to other domains, brands, or projects

Explainer pages prioritize **clarity, transparency, and factual accuracy**.

---

## Core Principles

All explainer pages in this repository should follow these principles.

### 1. Factual
Content should focus on verifiable facts rather than promotional language.

### 2. Transparent
Pages should clearly explain the organization or project being described without ambiguity about ownership or purpose.

### 3. Neutral Tone
Explainer pages should avoid advertising language or exaggerated claims.

### 4. Simple Format
Pages should remain simple HTML documents that are easy to read, preserve, and archive long-term.

### 5. Public Reference
Each page should link to the official website or primary domain of the organization being described.

---

## Standard Page Structure

Explainer pages should generally follow this structure:

### Title
The name of the organization or project.

### Overview
A short explanation of what the entity is.

### Background
Relevant historical or contextual information.

### Services or Activities
What the organization does.

### Ownership or Leadership
When appropriate, the individuals responsible for the organization.

### External Links
Links to official websites or related resources.
